Research Experience

Fault Detection in Robotic Swarms | Prof. Giovanni Beltrame

Duration: May 2018 - Jul' 2018

As a summer research intern at the Making Innovative Space Technology Lab (MISTLAB), at
Ecole Polytechnique de Montreal, Canada. I was working with Vivek Shankar Varadharajan (Ph.D. Candidate).
The work by Tarapore Danesh, Anders Lyhne Christensen, & Jon Timmis “Generic, scalable and decentralized
fault detection for robot swarms.” have adopted the cross regulation of the adaptive immune system of vertebrates
for multi-agent robotic systems and used it to dectect faults in robotic swarms.

We are working to build on their work to come up with a strategy to classify the detected faults on a swarm of
KheperaIVs. I integrated the cross regulation model developed for E-pucks with the Khepera IV ARGoS3 Model.
We intend to the extend the study to include more types of anomalies for differenr robots and environments.

Spherical Tele-operated Robot | Prof. Leena Vachhani & Prof. Dipti Gupta

Duration: Dec' 2017 - May' 2018

At the Embedded Controls Lab, the spherical robot was developed previously. It is currently operated using
an android application. The autonomous operation of the robot required development of a control loop and a
strategy for outdoor state estimation. I was working to develop the later using an inertial measurement unit
with WiFi. Additionally, we intended to make the robot throwable and it required an appropriate mateiral
selection. With the PEEL Lab headed by Prof. Dipti Gupta at IIT-B, I performed experiments to study the effects of
temperature & composition on the tensile strength of the material to be used for fabricating the shell of the robot.

This was my Bachelor's Thesis project and required me to do a thorough literature survey before
experimenting in the lab. The experimentation involved motion analysis using VICON motion capture systems
and testing of mechanical properties of the material films.

Setup of Spiri - Drone by Pleaides Robotics

Duration: May' 2018 - Jul' 2018

Spiri is an autonomous drone equipped with Nvidia TX1/TX2 and stereo vision cameras that uses the Pixracer
flight controller. The on-board computer runs an Ubuntu 16.04 with ROS-Kinetic. The internship at Mistlab
involved setting up of the drone for formation flights along with other drones already being used in the lab.

The setup required loading the TX1 with the necessary packages including ROSBuzz. A Hardware-in-the-loop
simulation (HITL) followed to test the real flight controller hardware with the actual flight code(ROSBuzz).
Gazebo is connected to QGroundControl(Mission Planning software) via UDP that bridges its MAVLink
messages to PX4. Later, the Mavros/Custom Modes for the PX4 flight stack were figured out for the ROSBuzz node.

Hydrogen Peroxide Ethanol Rover (HYPER) | Prof. Arindrajit Chowdhury

Duration: Sept' 2016 - Apr' 2017

Drone survillence is a field that has seen a lot of growth in the past decade. A drone that can disguise itself in the environment
would fit the application well. A flapping wing drone would serve this purpose quite aptly. We formed a team in Sept'16 to
develop an Ornithoptor. The use of a mono-propellant as fuel was being developed in Prof. Chowdhury's lab. We intended to
use this method to flap the wings of the drone to generate thrust.

I was primarily working on the fabrication of the Ornithopter which involved design and manufacture of wings. We made large
wings of 1m span with S-1020 airfoil. The next step was to make these giant wings flap. We made a crank-rocker mechanism
based gear box for power tranmission and initially flapped wings with the help of a battery. The flapping initially was only an
up-down motion of the huge wings. Meanwhile, we needed a method to measure the thrust generated by this flapping. We
manufactured a 'Test-Rig' to measure the generated thrust using a s-type load cell. In the later stages, I was wokring on
developing a strategy to control the wings. I directed my efforts to measure the precise position of Wings in space using hall
sensors to feed it to the fed micro-controller and adjust the servo motors to adjust the angle of attack and torsion in the wing.

Rakshak, Student Technical Team | Prof. Aditya A. Paranjape

Duration: Sept' 2016 - Dec' 2016

Rakshak is a student technical team that works to develop an Unmanned Aerial Vehicles for Aero Design Competition
organised by the Society of Automotive Engineers-International, USA. I was recruited as part of the 40 membered student
team. At the Aerodynamics subsystem, we analysed and designed different factors to ensure a stable flight and best possible

Working with these two teams, I got an insight of the importance of control in robotics and wanted to study the field. I took up
my first course in Spring'17. Later, I started working on acquainting myself with Robotics & Controls.

Course Projects

Certain courses in my curriculum required me to complete a project extending over a month or so.
These projects carry a 10%-20% weightage of the total credits for the course.

Controller Design of an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle | Adaptive Control Theory, Prof. Srikant Sukumar

    For the course on Adaptive Control Theory, our team of 3 studied the formulation of the adaptive backstepping method and
    it's implementation to control the longitudinal flight dynamics (Flight Path Angle & Veloctiy) of a fixed wing UAV under the
    constraint of thrust saturation as presented in the scientific paper by F. Gavilan et. al., “Adaptive Control for Aircraft Longitudinal
    Dynamics with Thrust Saturation”, JOURNAL OF GUIDANCE, CONTROL, AND DYNAMICS Vol. 38, No. 4, April 2015    .

Mission Design for Atlas-Centaur SLV-3D rocket | Spaceflight Mechanics, Prof. Ashok Joshi

    The Einstein Observatory, HEAO-2, the first fully imaging X-Ray Telescope in space was launched on an Atlas-Centaur SLV-3D
    booster rocket into a near-circular orbit. For the course on Spaceflight Mechanics, I reproduced the basic burnout profile including
    the launch and maneuver trajectories of the rocket and orbital decay of the satellite after the mission.

Analysis of Mechanical Properties of Bones | Mechanics of Materials, Prof. Aparna Singh

    The course on Mechanical Properties of Materials required us to study the behavior of bones under forces such as tensile & sheer stresses,
    compression & fatigue. It involved the examination of properties of bones due to their material composition and mechanical structure.

Analysis of Point of Purchase of Packed Food | Data Analysis & Interpretation, Prof. Shobha Shukla

    In a team of 4, we surveyed a total of 18 eateries in the vicinity of our IIT-B campus. We compiled a data on the consumer behavior,
    sales' statistics and nutritional data of around 126 packed food good to establish a relationship in the mentioned variables.
    We consilated a brief report commenting on the point of purchase of these packed goods.
